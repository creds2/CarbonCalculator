This tool helps show how and why peopleâ€™s carbon footprint varies across England. It was developed at the University of Leeds in partnership with Transport for the South East and funding from the Centre for Research into Energy Demand Solutions

We have used a wide range of data to estimate the carbon footprint of each Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) in England. LSOAs are statistical areas with about 1,500 people. Although the estimates are based on the best aviaible data for each area, they are averages for areas and so may not be an accurate representation of any particular indervidual.

A placed-based tool, like this, is best for understanding structural issues that affect whole communities. For example, people in a certain area may drive a lot becuase they don't have access to public transport.

Use the drop-down menu to view different datasets or click on an area to get a detailed report card.

Travel to Work Mode Share (2011 Cenus)

The main mode of travel to work from the 2011 Census. Although this data is now quite old its is very sptailly detailed and so still useful. Commuting makes up about 20% of trips in England, so it is a minority of trips and distnace travlled, yet ther is some evidence that how people choose to travel to work affects their general travel choices. Comparison with the 2001 Census sugges that commuter travel patterns are reasonably stable. However to meet climate change targets we need more people to travel by active travel (walking and cycling) and public transport.
